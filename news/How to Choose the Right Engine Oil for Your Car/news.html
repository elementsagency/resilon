Choosing the right engine oil is one of the most important decisions you can make to keep your car running smoothly. With so many types of oils available, it can feel confusing—especially when you’re faced with numbers like 5W-30 or 0W-20. In this guide, we’ll break down everything UK drivers need to know about engine oil and why Resilon is the smart choice for long-lasting performance.

Why Engine Oil Matters

Engine oil is more than just lubrication. It:

- Reduces friction and wear on engine parts
- Keeps the engine cool by carrying away heat
- Prevents deposits and sludge build-up
- Protects against corrosion
- Improves fuel efficiency
- Using the wrong oil can lead to increased wear, higher emissions, and even engine damage.

Step 1: Check Your Vehicle Handbook

The first rule when choosing oil is simple: always check your vehicle’s handbook. Your manufacturer will list the exact oil grade and specifications your car requires. This ensures your engine is protected and your warranty remains valid.

Step 2: Understand Oil Grades (e.g., 5W-30)

Oil grades describe how oil flows at different temperatures. For example:

- 5W-30: Flows well in cold starts (5W) and provides reliable protection at normal operating temperature (30).
- 0W-20: Very thin oil for newer engines, focused on fuel economy.

UK drivers often need oils like 5W-30 or 5W-40, as they suit both colder winters and moderate summers.

Step 3: Look for OEM Approvals

Not all oils are created equal. The best products meet or exceed OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) approvals from brands like BMW, Mercedes, Volkswagen, and GM.

Resilon Premium Oils, for example, are OEM-approved and dexos2 certified, making them compatible with a wide range of modern petrol and diesel cars in the UK.

Step 4: Consider Your Driving Style

- City driving Frequent stop-start trips demand oils with strong anti-wear protection.
- Motorway driving Long-distance drivers benefit from oils that support extended service intervals.
- High-performance cars Require fully synthetic oils that withstand high temperatures and stress.

Resilon oils are engineered for all driving conditions, from urban commutes to high-performance motorway driving.

Step 5: Synthetic vs. Semi-Synthetic Oils

- Fully Synthetic Oils (like Resilon 5W-30) provide the best protection, fuel efficiency, and engine cleanliness.
- Semi-Synthetic Oils are cheaper but less durable.

For most modern UK vehicles, fully synthetic oil is the best choice.

Final Tip: Don’t Forget Regular Oil Changes

Even the best oil needs to be replaced. Most UK cars require an oil change every 6,000–10,000 miles (10,000–15,000 km) or once a year. Always follow your manufacturer’s service schedule.
